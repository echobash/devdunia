🧠 HOW BASE32 WORKS EXPLAINER
1

Every character becomes its byte value (UTF-8 encoding). "H" = 72 = 01001000.

2

Bytes are grouped in 5-bit chunks instead of 8. Each chunk maps to one of 32 characters.

3

The alphabet is A-Z plus 2-7 — no confusing characters like 0, 1, 8, or 9.

4

= padding is added to make output length a multiple of 8. Base32 is ~60% larger than the original.

5

Base32 is case-insensitive and avoids ambiguous characters — perfect for human input like OTP secrets.
